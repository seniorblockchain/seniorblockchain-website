The Blockcore uses a Proof of Stake (PoS) algorithm to validate transactions, so owners of SBC can decide to stake their SBC. Once you are staking SBC , you can write blocks to the blockchain and earn SBC doing this. Importantly, the SBC Wallet is dual purpose; it enables you to stake your SBC in addition to making transactions. Staking helps you strengthen the blockchain network on which your SBC Tokens are held. A full copy of the SBC blockchain is held on every device the SBC wallet is running on, so the wallet is effectively sitting on top of a Blockcore Full Node. When the Full Node detects SBC Tokens have been staked, it actively mines thereby contributing to the overall power and resilience of the network.

Case study: Proof of Work

Because even small stakes have a chance to mine, staking democratizes the mining process when compared with Proof of Work solutions. To have any chance of earning rewards from mining on a mature Proof of Work blockchain, considerable expenditure on hardware and energy is required.
